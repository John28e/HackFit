HACKATHON TRACKS

Problem Statements

Choose a challenge. Build something impactful. Innovate with purpose.

1

Bridging the Skill Gap in the Age of AI

A growing skill gap exists between what learners demonstrate academically and the competencies required by industry. While AI tools have become integral to learning and project development, over-reliance on AI-generated outputs can limit hands-on understanding and make it difficult to accurately assess actual skills and readiness.

The challenge is to design a system that identifies skill gaps, evaluates true competency, and supports effective skill development aligned with current and emerging workforce needs. The solution should help individuals, educators, or institutions close these gaps and improve employability.

AI & Education Skill Assessment Employability Workforce Readiness
2

Enterprise Sustainability through Smart Resource and Utility Management

Large organizations consume vast amounts of energy, water, and materials, making sustainability a complex and critical challenge. Achieving measurable and continuous resource optimization at an enterprise scale requires intelligent, data-driven approaches.

The challenge is to design an open-source, scalable solution that enables large enterprises to monitor, analyze, and optimize resource usage, supporting long-term sustainability goals. Participants are expected to identify a specific enterprise sustainability challenge, study real-world practices, and develop a functional system that drives responsible resource usage and informed decision-making.

Sustainability Resource Management Enterprise Scale Open Source Data-Driven
3

Game-Based Learning for Social (also called as sustainable) Development Goals

Awareness and understanding of social development challenges often remain limited when conveyed through traditional instructional methods. Engaging, interactive approaches are needed to help learners meaningfully understand real-world social issues and the impact of individual and collective actions.

The challenge is to design and develop a game-based system that educates players about Social Development Goals by embedding real-world scenarios, decision-making, and consequences within gameplay. The solution should promote learning through interaction, encourage critical thinking, and create awareness of social responsibility while maintaining engagement and playability.

Game-Based Learning Social Impact SDGs Interactive Education Awareness

HACKF!T INTEL BRIEFING

Everything teams keep pinging us about the sprint, power, and prize stack—decoded in one signal burst.

HackFit is open exclusively to undergraduate and postgraduate students currently enrolled in recognized colleges or universities. Participants must carry a valid student ID. Mixed teams across different colleges are welcome, provided all members are eligible students and ready to build on-site.
HackFit 2026 features three tracks: Bridging the Skill Gap in the Age of AI, Enterprise Sustainability through Smart Resource and Utility Management, and Game-Based Learning for Social Development Goals. Each track comes with a detailed problem statement and judging criteria.
Teams can have between 2 to 5 members. Solo participation is not allowed. Cross-college teams are encouraged — diversity in skills and backgrounds makes for stronger solutions.
On-site support includes dedicated mentors for each track, technical infrastructure, food and refreshments throughout the 36-hour sprint, and a helpdesk for any logistical needs. Workstations and power outlets will be available at all times.
Projects are judged on innovation and creativity, technical complexity, real-world impact, completeness and presentation quality, and track alignment. Each team will demo their project to a panel of industry experts and faculty evaluators at the end of the sprint.
